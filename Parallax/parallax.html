Health Benefits

Studies suggest that durian fruit offers the following health benefits (2Trusted Source, 3Trusted Source): Reduces cancer risk. Its antioxidants may neutralize cancer-promoting free radicals. In one test-tube study, durian extract prevented a strain of breast cancer cells from spreading. Prevents heart disease. Fights infection. The rind contains compounds that have antibacterial and anti-yeast properties. Durian has a lower glycemic index (GI) than many other tropical fruits, meaning it may spike blood sugar levels less.

DURIAN VARIETIES

Arancillo

Arancillo combines the strong flavor and bitternesss of the native with slightly thicker flesh and a smooth, silky texture. It is apparently a seedling of the Thai Chanee, discovered by former BPI-PhilFruits director Vicente Arancillo. While it is one of the preferred durians by taste, Arancillo has a tendency to rot in heavy rain and is not a recommended variety for export.

ARANCILLO

Duyaya

A bright yellow durian with a strange, elongated shape very like the Petruk durian from Java. It’s actually the seedling of a Monthong, and is reliably sweet. The name Duyaya is a combination of “Durian” and “Bisaya” – the ethnic tribe of Calinan farmer Severino Belviz. Edible portion: 32%.

DUYAYA

Puyat

Puyat has a beautiful yellow-orange coloration and a very thick, bitter-sweet flesh. It is one of the largest durians, recognizable by its large brown spikes, pear shape, and thick stem. Like Arancillo, it is a seedling of a Chanee, brought to the Philippines in the 1970’s by a Mr. Puyat. When the Singapore Fruits and Vegetable Association visited Davao this year, out of twelve selected varieties Puyat was their favorite. Since the tree is also resistant to phytophtera and other types of fungus, it is currently the favored variety among farmers. Edible portion: 40%.

PUYAT

Kob or D99

Registered as D99 in Malaysia, Kob is originally from Thailand. In the Philippines, it’s one of the most popular durian varieties. It comes in two colors depending on the altitude, yellow or white, and is reliably chocolate-sweet with a thick flesh. It is one of the durians considered for export.

KOB / D99

D101

Malaysian durian varieties, such as D101, are just beginning to filter into the Philippines. While rare, D101 is the most popular because of its thick, buttery flesh and frosting-like sweetness.
